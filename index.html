An epidemiological trend analysis of oral cancer in Korea from 2001 to 2021.
BMC Oral Health (SCIE), 2025.

This nationwide epidemiological study analyzed 20 years of oral cancer incidence and mortality data from the Korean National Cancer Registry. I contributed to data visualization, statistical analysis, and figure development, with a focus on age-, sex-, and subsite-specific trends in oral cancer. Dental & Oral Health Research

Disparities in Access to Dental Sealants Among People with Disabilities in Korea

I conducted a nationwide population-based analysis using National Health Insurance Service claims data from 2013 to 2023 to evaluate disparities in access to dental sealants among children and adolescents with disabilities. The study quantified preventive care utilization using procedure codes for disabled and non-disabled populations and examined differences by disability status, gender, and region.

The analysis revealed persistently lower sealant utilization among individuals with disabilities, with disabled females accessing preventive care at substantially lower rates than disabled males. Significant regional disparities were also observed, with markedly reduced access outside the capital metropolitan area. These findings underscore structural inequities in preventive dentistry and support the need for targeted public health and policy interventions for vulnerable populations.

Digital Oral Health Management for Elderly Populations

In collaboration with the School of Dentistry, Seoul National University, I developed a digital oral health management application for elderly users integrating validated self-assessment tools (GOHAI, OHSE-K). The system was designed to support preventive oral healthcare through usability-focused design, personalized feedback, and structured self-monitoring, with an emphasis on accessibility and health literacy.

Biomedical & Translational Research

Glioma cfDNA Classification (2024–2025)

At the Biophotonics and Nano Engineering Lab, Seoul National University, I developed a deep learning–based pipeline to enhance detection of glioma cell-free DNA from RRBS methylation data. The project addressed extreme class imbalance and low signal-to-noise conditions by integrating Bis-SNP–based SNV labeling and downstream model refinement, demonstrating the applicability of computational methods to translational biomedical research.
